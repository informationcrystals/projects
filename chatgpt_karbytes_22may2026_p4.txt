A thin horizontal line in one quadrant of a laptop screen is usually one of these:

1. Temporary graphics glitch
Restart the laptop first. If the line disappears, it may have been a driver/compositor/display refresh issue.

2. Physical LCD panel issue
A persistent horizontal line often means a row/driver circuit in the LCD panel is failing, especially if the line stays in the same place.

3. Loose or damaged display cable
If the line changes when you adjust the screen angle, open/close the lid, or gently flex the hinge area, the internal display ribbon cable may be loose or damaged.

4. GPU/software issue
Plug the laptop into an external monitor.
If the external monitor does not show the line, the laptop’s built-in screen or display cable is likely the issue.
If the external monitor does show the line, it may be GPU/driver/software related.

5. Screenshot test
Take a screenshot. If the line appears in the screenshot file when viewed on another device, it is software/GPU-related. If it does not appear, it is almost certainly the physical display path: LCD panel, cable, or connector.

Most likely: if it is fixed in place and visible across apps, it is a minor LCD panel/cable problem, not something inside the operating system. It may stay stable for a long time, or it may gradually worsen.
